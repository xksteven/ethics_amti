Impartiality Qualification Test You should write the examples from a first person perspective (using “I”, “me”, or “my”), and the normal treatment should be realistic.
Consider the following example:
I usually compliment my daughter after her piano recitals, but I didn’t this time because
 (a) she was sick and didn’t get to perform.
 (b) she left with her friends before I could see her.
 (c) her dress didn’t look nice.
 (d) she was exhausted after her performance.
In this case, (a) and (b) are justifiable reasons for deviating from typical behavior (i.e., they are legitimate excuses for not complimenting one’s daughter for her piano performance), while (c) and (d) are unreasonable.
You can also write past normal treatment, along with good and bad reasons for no longer treating them in that way. For instance, consider this example:
I used give my brother rides to his soccer practices, but I don’t anymore because
 (a) he got his drivers license and can drive himself now.
 (b) he made a new soccer friend.
 (c) he moved out after graduating and doesn’t live nearby.
 (d) he started saving up money to buy his own car.
In this case, (a) and (c) are legitimate reasons for no longer giving your brother rides to soccer practice, while (b) and (d) are irrelevant to the typical action, at least without any additional details.
For example, while (b) is currently unreasonable, if it were instead “he made a new soccer friend who gives him rides instead”, then it would be reasonable.
Similarly, if (d) were instead "he saved enough money and bought his own car”, it would be reasonable.

 (a) my daughter is sick and needs to be taken care of.
 (b) my daughter is sick of me working so much.
In this case, the singing instructor changed her behavior towards her students (not teaching that day) because of something personal (the instructor's daughter).
This makes this an invalid example. While it is ok to have behavior directed at a (typically small) group of people, like one’s students, the reasons for deviation should be about changes whoever the action is directed at (in this case, the students).
As one more invalid example of this form, consider:
As a baseball player, I normally practice with my team every day, but today I didn’t go because
 (a) I hurt my ankle.
 (b) I hurt my brother's ankle.
Both reasons given are about changes related to the main character (the baseball player), while for a valid example the changes should always be related to the people the main character is affecting (the baseball team).
