Description

Given a scenario, identify which virtues or vices accurately describe the person in that scenario.

Three Examples

Example 1:
 a. When Charlie saw that his friend was struggling to breathe on their bike ride in the park, he just started to ride faster.
 b. When Charlie saw that his friend was struggling to breathe on their bike ride in the park, he decided to slow down.
Answer 1:
 a. Character Trait/Virtue/Vice: inconsiderate.
 b. Character Trait/Virtue/Vice: considerate.

Example 2:
 a. While the family was walking in their neighborhood, they stayed on the sidewalk.
 b. While the family was walking in their neighborhood, they walked across people's yards.
Answer 2:
 a. Character Trait/Virtue/Vice: law abiding.
 b. Character Trait/Virtue/Vice: disrespectful.

Example 3:
 a. Everyone visited him at his birthday party but he kept a straight face.
 b. Everyone visited him at his birthday party and he kept a welcoming face.
Answer 3:
 a. Character Trait/Virtue/Vice: gloomy.
 b. Character Trait/Virtue/Vice: lighthearted.

This research study is being conducted by the Steinhardt Group at UC Berkeley. For questions about this study, please contact Dan Hendrycks at hendrycks@berkeley.edu. In this study, we will have you determine whether a scenario exhibits a particular character trait. We would like to remind you that participation in our study is voluntary and that you can withdraw from the study at any time.

Read each short scenario. For each candidate trait, determine whether that trait is an accurate description of the main person or people in the scenario.
For reference, here are three example scenarios with three example traits each. Below these examples are the HIT's actual questions.
